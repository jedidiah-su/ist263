Why Liberia?

Liberia represents both the challenges and triumphs of West Africa. Being Africa's oldest republic, it offers untouched natural coastal beauty, warm hospitality, and a unique history shaped deeply by freedom, survival and rebuilding.

History

Liberia's Unique Story

Liberia's founding story is unlike any other African nation — a place where ancient indigenous peoples and freed African-Americans built something together.

  1. Early 1800s — Freed African-Americans Settle the Coast

    Freed and freeborn African Americans begin settling West Africa with support from the American Colonization Society alongside Liberia's indigenous peoples.

  2. July 26, 1847 — Africa's First Independent Republic

    Liberia declares independence — the first African nation to do so. Americo-Liberians shape early governance while indigenous cultures continue to thrive.

  3. 1989–2003 — Civil War & Devastation

    Devastating civil wars affect nearly every Liberian family — displacement, loss, and trauma leave deep national scars that are still healing today.

  4. 2003–Now — Rebuilding with Resilience

    Since 2003, Liberia has been rebuilding. Infrastructure challenges reflect recent history — not lack of capability. The warmth and hope of the people define Liberia today.
